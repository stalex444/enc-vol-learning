## üí° Key Teaching: The Protocol

### Types of Seizures

**TONIC-CLONIC (Grand Mal) - Most Dramatic**:
- Sudden loss of consciousness, falls if standing
- Body rigid (tonic phase) 10-20 seconds
- Rhythmic jerking (clonic phase) 1-3 minutes
- Eyes roll back, jaw clenches
- May bite tongue, lose bladder control
- Breathing irregular during seizure
- Post-ictal: confused, disoriented, 5-30 min recovery

**FOCAL (Partial) - More Subtle**:
- May remain conscious but "off"
- Staring, unresponsive, repetitive movements
- 1-2 minutes, slowly returns to normal

**ABSENCE - Very Brief**:
- Sudden blank stare, 5-10 seconds
- Returns immediately, may not realize it happened

**MYOCLONIC - Quick Jerks**:
- Brief jerks/twitches, person stays conscious
- Very brief‚Äîa second or less

**Focus of this chapter: TONIC-CLONIC** (most dramatic, clearest protocol needed)

---

### The 7-Step Protocol

**STEP 1: STAY CALM AND ASSESS (5 seconds)**
- Confirm it's a seizure
- Note the time it started
- Quick safety scan

**STEP 2: PROTECT FROM INJURY (Immediate)**
- Ease to ground if falling
- Clear space around them
- Move objects that could cause injury
- ‚ùå Do NOT restrain or put anything in mouth

**STEP 3: GET HELP (Immediate)**
- Signal another volunteer: "Medical emergency‚Äîseizure. Get Medical NOW."
- Post in Telegram with location and time
- ‚ùå Do NOT leave them alone

**STEP 4: MONITOR AND TIME (During seizure)**
- Stay with them, keep timing
- Observe breathing (may be irregular‚Äînormal during seizure)
- Watch for injury
- Keep others back
- ‚ùå Do NOT restrain

**STEP 5: POSITION FOR SAFETY (When jerking stops)**
- Check breathing
- If breathing normally: turn on side (recovery position)
- If NOT breathing: DO NOT MOVE‚ÄîMedical handles airway
- If suspected spinal injury: DO NOT MOVE

**STEP 6: POST-ICTAL CARE (After seizure, before Medical)**
- Talk calmly: "You're safe. You had a seizure. Help is coming."
- Keep on side
- Loosen tight clothing
- ‚ùå Don't give food, water, or medication
- ‚ùå Don't let them walk around

**STEP 7: BRIEF MEDICAL (When they arrive)**
- Duration (start to stop time)
- Description of what you saw
- Any injuries
- Breathing status
- Level of consciousness
- Any known medical history

### CRITICAL: What NEVER to Do

**‚ùå NEVER put anything in their mouth**
- Myth: They'll swallow their tongue
- Truth: Physically impossible
- Danger: Breaks teeth, causes choking, obstructs airway

**‚ùå NEVER restrain their movements**
- Myth: Need to stop the jerking
- Truth: Seizure must run its course
- Danger: Causes muscle/bone injuries, doesn't stop seizure

**‚ùå NEVER give water, food, or medication during/immediately after**
- Myth: They need water or meds
- Truth: Can't swallow safely
- Danger: Choking, aspiration into lungs

**‚ùå NEVER move them unless in immediate danger**
- Myth: Need to be comfortable
- Truth: Movement can cause injury
- Danger: Worsens spinal injuries, complicates assessment

**‚ùå NEVER leave them alone**
- Myth: They need privacy
- Truth: They need monitoring
- Danger: Another seizure, stop breathing, aspirate vomit

**‚ùå NEVER assume they're okay immediately after**
- Myth: Seizure's over, they're fine
- Truth: Post-ictal state lasts 30+ min, needs evaluation
- Danger: Complications, underlying cause needs assessment

**The Principle: First, do no harm.**

**Your job**:
1. Protect from injury
2. Time it
3. Get Medical
4. Monitor
5. Stay calm

**That's it. Everything else is unnecessary or dangerous.**

---

### When to Call 911

**CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY IF**:
1. Seizure lasts longer than 5 minutes (status epilepticus)
2. Second seizure before full recovery (cluster seizures)
3. Seizure occurred in water
4. Person injured in fall (head trauma, spinal injury, broken bones)
5. Not breathing normally after seizure stops
6. First-time seizure (no epilepsy history)
7. Person is pregnant
8. Person is diabetic

**LET MEDICAL HANDLE**:
- Known epilepsy, <5 min, breathing resumes normally
- No injuries, Medical present and assessing
- Person returns to baseline within expected timeframe

**Your role**: Provide information so Medical can decide. If 5+ minutes and Medical not there, or not breathing post-seizure‚Äîyou call 911.

---

### Post-Seizure Care

**Post-ictal state**: Brain recovering, person not fully conscious/oriented, 5-30+ minutes

**What you'll observe**:
- Confusion, disorientation
- Sleepiness or exhaustion
- Possible agitation/aggression (not personal)
- Headache, muscle soreness
- Slurred speech, memory gaps
- Emotional (crying, fear, embarrassment)

**Your role**:
1. **Maintain safety positioning**: Keep on side, support head, don't let them stand
2. **Provide calm reassurance**: "You're safe. You had a seizure. You're going to be okay."
3. **Protect dignity**: Keep onlookers back, cover if needed, no photos
4. **Monitor for complications**: Watch breathing, check for injuries, note improvement
5. **Don't give them anything**: No water, food, meds until Medical clears
6. **Stay with them**: Until Medical arrives and takes over

**When Medical arrives, report**:
- Total duration
- Description of seizure activity
- Time seizure ended
- Current level of consciousness
- Any injuries
- Medical history if known
- Current status vs. right after (improving/same/worse)

---

### Documentation

**Write down immediately**:

**Timing**:
- Start time (precise)
- Stop time (when jerking ended)
- Total duration
- Time Medical took over

**Description**:
- What you saw (facts, not interpretations)
- Sounds they made
- Eye position, skin color

**Injuries**:
- Hit head? On what?
- Bit tongue/cheek?
- Other visible injuries

**Context**:
- What they were doing when it started
- Any warning signs mentioned
- Had they said they weren't feeling well

**Post-seizure**:
- How long until responsive
- Any repeated seizures
- Status when Medical took over

**What to tell Medical**: Everything above, clearly and concisely. Start with most critical.

**What NOT to do**:
- Don't post on social media
- Don't discuss details with other participants
- Don't gossip
- Don't speculate about cause
- This person's medical event is private
